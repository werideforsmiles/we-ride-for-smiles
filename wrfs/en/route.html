The 2027 route

Paris → Edinburgh · Departure planned for 14 June 2027

Over 1,500 km by bike, across France and the United Kingdom, to support sick children and their families.

The route shown is provisional. Stages and distances may evolve depending on traffic conditions, logistics, and the partners who join the adventure.

Photo by Dimitri Iakymuk on Unsplash

1

Paris → Rouen

125 km

The challenge begins in the heart of Paris. This first day will take us to Rouen along the Seine valley, with more than 1,500 kilometres still ahead.

Photo by Barry Talley on Unsplash

2

Rouen → Le Havre

95 km

Toward the Normandy coast and Le Havre — the last French stage before crossing the Channel.

Photo by Paul-François Gapais on Unsplash

3

Le Havre → Portsmouth → Southampton

Sea crossing, then about 30 km by bike

After the crossing to Portsmouth, we will ride on to Southampton. A shorter day on the bike, but essential to make the transition from France to England.

Photo by Hasan Karaman on Unsplash

4

Southampton → London

135 km

A long day toward the British capital, from the roads of southern England into the heart of London.

Photo by Benjamin Davies on Unsplash

5

Rest day · London

Recovery and meetings

A first day to recover, maintain the bikes, and meet partners and supporters of the project.

Photo by Samuel Dixon on Unsplash

6

London → Oxford

105 km

We will leave London for Oxford and begin our progress toward the west of England.

Photo by Ben Seymour on Unsplash

7

Oxford → Bath

110 km

A rolling stage through the English countryside and the Cotswolds, to the city of Bath.

Photo by Elena Jiang on Unsplash

8

Bath → Cardiff

100 km

Heading for Wales, crossing the Severn estuary before reaching Cardiff.

Photo by Taylor Floyd Mews on Unsplash

9

Cardiff → Birmingham

170 km

One of the longest days of the route. We leave Wales for Birmingham, in the heart of the Midlands.

Photo by Gabriel McCallin on Unsplash

10

Rest day · Birmingham

Recovery and logistics

A second pause for the team to recover, service the gear, and prepare for the final part of the challenge.

Photo by Lulu Black on Unsplash

11

Birmingham → Manchester

145 km

The road resumes toward north-west England, all the way to Manchester.

Photo by Mangopear creative on Unsplash

12

Manchester → York

115 km

A demanding stage across northern England, toward the historic city of York.

Photo by Jeffrey Zhang on Unsplash

13

York → Newcastle

155 km

We continue north to Newcastle — the last major stage before the Scottish border.

Photo by Karl Moran on Unsplash

14

Newcastle → Edinburgh

225 km · Longest stage

The final day on the bike will also be the longest. More than 200 kilometres along the east coast will separate us from the finish in Edinburgh.

After more than 1,500 kilometres, every participant, partner, and donor will have helped carry the project to its destination.

Photo by Adam Wilson on Unsplash
